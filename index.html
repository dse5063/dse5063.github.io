About this Guide

Content

In this guide there are resources to help students in nuclear engineering for their present and future endeavors. There are three sections to website. The first outlines the professional sites in nuclear engineering. The second looks at websites that can help students with homework. The third section adds a ..journal, forum page for nukes, and news about the industry. For each resource there is a simple summary of what each website is for and how to use it easily.

Audience

This internet resource guide is intended for undergraduate students in Nuclear Engineering at The Pennsylvania State University. Students in their first two years may not find the information as appealing, but there is enough to understand the nuclear field and get help if needed. Students in their final two years most likely know about the professional sites and homework help; however, the other resources can help keep in touch with the nuclear field and ask other nukes questions.

What should be known?

Nuclear engineering undergrads should have learned the basics of engineering in the first 4 semesters at PSU. No nuclear classes are offered until junior year, but coming in with the knowledge of nuclear reactions, reactors, etc., would be a good idea.
